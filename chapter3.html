Chapter 3

Trigonometric Identities

We have seen a few trigonometry basic and principles in the previous chapter. Now we will take a look deeper to a trigonometric identities which has a lot of formula.

Phytagoras Theorem

sin 2 θ + cos 2 θ = 1

1 + cot 2 θ = cosec 2 θ

tan 2 θ + 1 = sec 2 θ

Compound-angle Formula

cos (A + B) = cos A cos B - sin A sin B

cos (A - B) = cos A cos B + sin A sin B

sin (A + B) = sin A cos B + cos A sin B

sin (A - B) = sin A cos B - cos A sin B

Cosecant, Secant, and Cotangent

From the image above, we can say:


sin (θ) = 1 / csc (θ)

cos (θ) = 1 / sec (θ)

tan (θ) = 1 / cot (θ)

And the other way around:


csc (θ) = 1 / sin (θ)

sec (θ) = 1 / cos (θ)

cot (θ) = 1 / tan (θ)
